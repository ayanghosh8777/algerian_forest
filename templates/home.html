Forest Fire Weather Index (FWI) Prediction

Enter the environmental parameters below to predict the Fire Weather Index (FWI), which indicates the potential intensity and spread of a forest fire.

Parameter Description

  • Temperature (°C) – Air temperature in degrees Celsius.
  • Rain (mm) – Amount of rainfall received.
  • FFMC (Fine Fuel Moisture Code) – Indicates the moisture content of surface litter and fine fuels.
    Typical Range: 18 – 96
  • DMC (Duff Moisture Code) – Indicates the moisture content of loosely compacted organic layers.
    Typical Range: 1 – 300+
  • DC (Drought Code) – Indicates long-term drying of deep compact organic matter.
    Typical Range: 7 – 800+
  • ISI (Initial Spread Index) – Measures the expected rate of fire spread.
    Higher value = Faster spread
  • BUI (Build Up Index) – Represents the total amount of fuel available for combustion.
  • Classes –
    • 0 = No Fire
    • 1 = Fire
  • Region –
    • 0 = Bejaia Region
    • 1 = Sidi-Bel Abbes Region

Interpretation:

  • FWI < 5: Very Low Fire Danger
  • 5 – 15: Low Fire Danger
  • 15 – 30: Moderate Fire Danger
  • 30 – 45: High Fire Danger
  • > 45: Extreme Fire Danger

A higher Fire Weather Index indicates a greater potential for fire ignition, faster fire spread, and increased fire intensity.
